These are the two most important questions if you wish to find a boat to board.

Indeed, for each crossing on a sailboat, the sea currents' directions and the main winds have to be studied. The sailboats choose their starting points depending on the most propice periods and places.

 Which direction do I have to choose ?

Most of the sailboats are travelling from East to West: this is why it's better to choose this direction.
Nevertheless, it's also possible to do the contrary. If you wish to do so, we advise you to try to cross the Atlantic from Europe or from Africa, and to cross the Pacific from America.

 How long does a crossing usually take ?

Although it depends on the time spent to each stop, it usually takes between 2 or 3 weeks to cross the Atlantic, and between 2 and 6 months to cross the Pacific.

N.B. : a sailboat approximately moves forward at five knots speed (9 km/h). Thanks to this indication, you have the possibility to estimate the time you'll need.
Also, don't forget to take into account whether or not the boat sails during the night or not.

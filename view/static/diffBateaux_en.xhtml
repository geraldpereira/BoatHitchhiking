Les différents types de bateaux - #{messages['meta.title']} Which kind of boat should I choose for a crossing ?

Bateaustop focus on . Indeed, these are the most common crafts.

Various reasons lead to publish :

- Offers published by amateur sailors
Amateur sailors are most of the time both owners of a boat and skipper. Travelling is a hobby for them, and they regularly need the help of shipmates during long or difficult journeys.

- Yacht delivery
Some boats'owners ask a skipper to bring their craft in a precise place. The skipper then employs shipmates to get help for this job. If the shipmates are skilled, they can be paid for it.

- Charters
A charter is a boat rented to several people during a specific period (which usually lasts a week). These crossings are a way for passengers to spend holidays: they often take advantage of the fact that a skipper and a steward are on board. Therefore, you have to pay for this kind of offer. Nevertheless, it's also possible to get hired for crossings such as these ones (as a waiter, a chef...).

It's also possible to use motorboats to do a crossing.
The boats transporting goods only accept the qualified hitchhikers. Nevertheless, sometimes cabins are specially equipped for passengers, but their cost is around 100€ per day! This is therefore conceivable only for short crossings, and only if you can't find a place or a period when sailboats are available. Furthermore, it's very rare when motorboats complete long crossings.
